## Understanding Authenticator App Integration

Integrating Robonito with a secure **Authenticator App Secret** allows the automation engine to generate precise Time-Based One-Time Passwords (TOTPs) natively, operating exactly like authentication apps such as Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or Authy. 

This feature is incredibly useful for automating test cases that involve tight **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** pipelines. Instead of getting blocked by a manual OTP screen, Robonito will securely generate the necessary OTP and dynamically inject it into your active test.

---

## Adding a New Authenticator Account

> **Note:** You can securely create and manage multiple authenticator app accounts within a single workspace.

To bind a new authenticator account, you must provide your service's **secret key**.

### Configuration Steps:

1. **Go to Integrations:** From your main Robonito dashboard, click **Integrations** located in the left sidebar.
2. **Select Authenticator App:** Locate the Authenticator App integration tile and click the `Add Account` button to begin the setup.
3. **Enter Connection Details:** A side drawer will open containing a configuration form. You must provide the following:
   - **Account Name:** A descriptive, identifiable name showing what exact site this 2FA setup belongs to (e.g., `GitHub 2FA`, `CRM Login OTP`).
   - **Secret Key:** The required secret authentication string provided directly by your 2FA service when you initially configure their 2FA protocols (e.g., the raw text key alternative provided alongside scanning a QR code).
4. **Advanced Options (Prefilled):** *In most standard use cases, you can leave these as their defaults.*
   - **Number of Digits:** The required length of the OTP (most commonly 6 or 8 digits).
   - **Period:** The interval threshold (in seconds) for which an OTP remains valid (typically 30 seconds).
   - **Algorithm:** The primary hashing algorithm used to securely generate the OTP (typically `SHA-1`).
5. **Add Account:** After providing the required fields, click `Save`. A system notification will actively confirm the success or failure of the connection.
6. **Confirm Integration:** Back on the Integrations page, the specific Authenticator App status will permanently update to `Connected` for your workspace.

## Recording Tests with Authenticator OTPs

> **Important:** You must add and successfully configure the respective authenticator account (as detailed above) before attempting to record an OTP step natively.

Once you have securely added an account, you can effortlessly generate and fetch live OTPs during an active test case execution.

### Execution Steps:

1. **Start Test Case:** Select the test case requiring a 2FA workflow and begin recording your automation steps.
2. **Navigate to OTP Page:** During your active recording, naturally navigate to the application screen that strictly requires the OTP verification layer.
3. **Fetch OTP:** Once the 2FA screen renders, locate your Robonito recording toolbar and click the **Fetch OTP** action button.
4. **Select Source:** An interaction dialog box will instantly appear. Select **Authenticator App** as your target OTP source.
5. **Select Account:** If you securely host multiple 2FA apps within your workspace, select the precise account name you wish to target from the dropdown menu.
6. **Auto-Fill Generation:** Once clicked, Robonito will algorithmically generate a valid code using the configured secret key, and automatically inject the populated OTP into the targeted verification field on the current page.
